Preventive Measures

If you had been diagnosed with breast cancer or have detected early signs of the condition, there are some factors that you can change to reduce the risk to a minimum and defeat the cancer.

Limit Alcohol

The more you drink, the greater your risk in developing breast cancer. General recommendation based on research, less than one drink a day

Don't smoke cigarettes

Research proves that there is an existing link between smoking and to breast cancer, especially in premenopausal women

Control your weight

Being overweight or obese increases risk of breast cancer especially, if obesity occurs latter in life

Be physically active

Physical activities help maintain healthy a weight, which helps prevent breast cancer. Aim to do at least 150 minutes a week of morderate activities

Breast Feeding

Breast feeding might play a role in breast cancer prevention.
The longer you breastfeed, the greater the protective effect

Limit dose and duration of hormorne therapy

Combination hormone therapy for more than three to five years increases the risk of breast cancer.

Avoid exposure to radiation and environment

Some research suggests a link between breast cancer and cumulative exposure to radiation over your lifetime. Reduce your exposure by having such tests only when absolutely necessary.
