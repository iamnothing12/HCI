A short introduction to Breast Cancer

What is Breast Cancer?

Breast cancer is a disease that is formed in the cells of the breast. There are different types of breast cancer and each type are dependent on which cells in the breast is affected.

In simple terms, a breast consists of three main parts:

  • Lobules: Glands that produce milk
  • Milk Ducts: Tubes that transport milk from the lobules to the nipples
  • Fats: A layer of fats that envelopes everything

Breast cancer commonly develops within the ducts or lobules and can infect the outer layer of the breast through blood vessels and lymph vessels. Cancers can potentially escalate and spread to other parts of the body, commonly known as metastasis.

Cancer Stages

Invasive Cancer

The cancer cells are growing larger but still contained within the breast but have affected nearby breast tissues. At this stage, to treat this disease, the following treatments may be considered:

Treatment:

  • Surgery
  • Combination Therapy (Hormone Therapy and/or Radiation Therapy and/or Chemotherapy)

Invasive Cancer

The cancer cells are growing larger but still contained within the breast but have affected nearby breast tissues. At this stage, to treat this disease, the following treatments may be considered:

Treatment:

  • Surgery
  • Combination Therapy (Hormone Therapy and/or Radiation Therapy and/or Chemotherapy)

Invasive Cancer

The cancer cells or tumor worsens and have spread to nearby muscles with evident swelling, inflamation or ulcers seen on the chest. At this stage, to treat this disease, the following treatments may be considered:

Treatment:

  • Chemotherapy
  • Radiation
  • Surgery

Severely Invasive Cancer

Cancer have spread to other organs (brain, lungs, bones etc) At this stage, one can only hope to depend on the following treatments to be considered:

Treatment:

  • A great Support team
  • Combination ( Chemotherapy / Radiation / Hormone Therapy)
  • Antidepressants
  • Anticonvulsants for pain or neurologic condition
  • Local anesthetics for pain
